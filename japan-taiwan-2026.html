LEE FAMILY TRAVEL LOG

Japan & Taiwan

June 18 – July 18, 2026

Tokyo

June 18–26 · Shinjuku

Stayed at Park Hyatt Tokyo

The trip began with the long haul from Los Angeles to Haneda and a week based in Shinjuku. Alexandra's good friend Mirei came up from Kobe to spend a day with us. Between the big-ticket attractions there were convenience-store runs, hotel-pool afternoons, and a lot of walking.

Home base was the Park Hyatt up in Shinjuku — unhurried breakfasts high above the city, and a nightcap at the New York Bar.

The kids ran their own city at KidZania in Toyosu, and we gave a full day to Toyosu again for teamLab Planets — the immersive water, light, and flower rooms.

A day in Kichijoji centered on Inokashira Park — first the swan boats on the pond, then the Ghibli Museum.

We spent the next morning at Meiji Jingu before a day trip down to Yokohama — the CupNoodles Museum, the harbor at Minato Mirai, and the Red Brick Warehouse. On the last morning, Alex and Cynthia tried kintsugi, the art of gold-seam repair, in Asakusa near Senso-ji.

Some of our food highlights: conveyor sushi at Sushiro, individual-booth ramen at Ichiran, bowls of udon at TsuruTonTan Ginza after teamLab, tonkatsu at Maisen in Aoyama, and yakitori at FUKU with Mirei. The best meal of the trip was the sukiyaki at Imahan in Shinjuku.

Hokkaido

June 26 – July 1 · Sapporo

Stayed at Courtyard by Marriott Sapporo

We flew north to Sapporo and hired a car for day trips across Hokkaido.

In Otaru, we saw the canal, the Music Box Museum, Kitaichi Glass, and LeTAO sweets. The next day was the flower country of Furano and Biei — the lavender rows at Farm Tomita and the otherworldly Blue Pond.

The next day took us to the volcanic Noboribetsu Hell Valley and its steaming Oyunuma pond, then on to Lake Toya and the Usu ropeway — with a roadside sika deer for good measure.

We spent the last day in Sapporo taking in the local sights. Sapporo meals included: burgers at Shogun, rich chicken-paitan ramen at Keimimansai, flat kishimen noodles at Kishimen no Kijiya, soup curry at Tiger Curry in Akarenga Terrace, and the meal highlight of Sapporo — Neapolitan pizza at Savoy Ezo.

Taipei

July 1–18 · Mandarin Oriental

Stayed at Mandarin Oriental Taipei

We spent the longest time in Taipei. The kids did summer camps, including a baking camp with Art Together, and a week at Shih Chien with tufting and pottery. Tim flew home early, but the rest of us stayed through the 17th.

Sightseeing filled the gaps between camp drop-offs: the Chiang Kai-shek and Sun Yat-sen memorial halls (with their hourly honor-guard changes), Taipei 101, the National Railway Museum, indoor golf at Golf Monsters, and an ice rink for good measure.

Taiwan meals never disappoint. Standouts: the Cantonese Ya Ge (雅閣) at the hotel, LILLA, Salt & Stone, and The Public House — where Tim caught up with his college friend Charles. Everyday favorites: fan tuan rice rolls at Wei Ding (味鼎), shave ice at Chun Mei (春美冰菓室), and an excellent foot massage from Ray at Mingyi (Dongmen). The city was full of familiar faces, too — Cynthia's college friend Charles, and old friends Homan and Angie — and at Chun Mei we met with Principal Wang enjoying her retirement in Taiwan.
